## Section 2: Cardiovascular Evidence — The Best-Replicated Signal

### 2.1 The Finding

Geomagnetic storms (high Kp) correlate with acute cardiovascular events (myocardial infarction, stroke onset) across multiple independent studies in multiple countries.

**Vencloviene et al. (2013)** — *International Journal of Biometeorology*, 57(5):793–801
- n = 6,071 MI cases, Lithuania, 5-year prospective study
- Geomagnetic storm days (Ap > 27) → OR = 1.12 (95% CI: 1.02–1.24) for MI onset
- Effect strongest in men and in patients without prior cardiac history

**Cornelissen et al. (2002)** — *Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy*, 56(Suppl 2):273s–283s
- Meta-analysis
- ~5–10% increase in MI incidence on days with Kp > 5 vs. quiet days

**Villoresi et al. (1994)** — *Journal of Geophysical Research*
- 20,000+ hospital cardiovascular admission records, Moscow and Rome
- Cosmic ray flux (inversely correlated with solar activity) tracked with admission rates

**Baris et al. (2008)** — Istanbul hospital data
- Statistically significant clustering of acute MI events during Kp > 4 days

These are independent research groups, different continents, different populations, different methodologies. They converge.
